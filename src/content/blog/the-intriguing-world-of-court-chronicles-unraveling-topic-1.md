## What is Judicial Review?

At its core, judicial review empowers courts to examine the actions of the legislative and executive branches, ensuring they align with the constitution. The landmark U.S. Supreme Court case *Marbury v. Madison* (1803) established this principle, with Chief Justice John Marshall declaring, "It is emphatically the province and duty of the judicial department to say what the law is."

Fast-forward to the present: Judicial review is invoked in debates over everything from election integrity to civil liberties.
