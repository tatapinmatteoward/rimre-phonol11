# Top 5 Rising Stars in the NBA for the 2024 Season

The NBA is always evolving, and with superstars aging or moving on, a new generation is stepping up. At Hoop Central, we're excited to spotlight five young players who could become household names this season. These athletes bring a mix of skill, athleticism, and potential that could reshape teams and rivalries.

## 1. Victor Wembanyama (San Antonio Spurs)

At just 20 years old, 'Wemby' is already a phenomenon. His 7'4" frame combined with guard-like skills makes him a matchup nightmare. Last season, he averaged 21.4 points, 10.6 rebounds, and 3.9 blocks. Expect him to push for MVP contention in 2024.

## 2. Chet Holmgren (Oklahoma City Thunder)

After missing his rookie year due to injury, Chet returned with a bang, posting 16.5 points, 7.9 rebounds, and 2.3 blocks per game. His shooting touch from deep sets him apart from traditional bigs. With OKC's young core, he's primed for a breakout.

## 3. Paolo Banchero (Orlando Magic)

The 2022 No. 1 pick has grown into a versatile forward, averaging 22.6 points and 6.9 rebounds last year. Banchero's scoring efficiency and playmaking could lead the Magic to the playoffs this season.

## 4. Jalen Williams (Oklahoma City Thunder)

'J-Dub' quietly became a star in 2023-24 with 19.1 points, 4.0 rebounds, and 4.5 assists. His smooth mid-range game and defensive prowess make him a two-way threat.

## 5. Scottie Barnes (Toronto Raptors)

The 2022 Rookie of the Year is evolving into a do-it-all wing. Averaging 19.9 points, 8.2 rebounds, and 6.1 assists, Barnes could be the cornerstone for Toronto's rebuild.
